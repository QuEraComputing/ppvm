Tutorials

Mid-length walkthroughs of ppvm's two simulation backends and the loss-aware Pauli basis they use. Heavier on motivation and theory than the Quick Start; lighter on end-to-end execution than the Examples notebooks.

  • Pauli Propagation

    Heisenberg-picture observable propagation with PauliSum: basic usage, compact and full Pauli notation, the two truncation knobs that control the approximation/performance trade-off, and the loss-aware LossyPauliSum variant.

  • Loss channel details

    The mathematical background for simulating qubit loss — the extended five-symbol Pauli basis (I, X, Y, Z, L), the Kraus operators of the loss channel, and how it interacts with downstream gates and measurement.

  • Generalized Stabilizer Tableau

    The Schrödinger-picture backend: stabilizer tableau extended with a sparse coefficient vector for non-Clifford gates, multi-shot sampling via fork(), executing Stim programs, and the noise channels available on top.
